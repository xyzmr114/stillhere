Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

What we collect

Still Here collects only what is necessary to operate the service:

  • Your email address and name (for account and check-in alerts)
  • Your daily check-in time preference
  • Emergency contact names, emails, and phone numbers you provide
  • Check-in history (timestamps only)
  • Device push notification tokens (stored only while the app is active)
  • Last active timestamp (to detect dormant accounts)

We do not collect location data, browsing history, or any data beyond what is needed to send check-in alerts.

How we use it

  • Sending your daily check-in notification at your chosen time
  • Alerting your emergency contacts if you miss a check-in (after your grace period)
  • Sending transactional emails (welcome, password reset, contact confirmations)
  • Detecting dormant accounts to pause escalations

We never sell your data. We never use it for advertising. We never share it with third parties except to deliver the service (Twilio for SMS, Resend for email, Firebase for push).

Emergency contacts

When you add an emergency contact, we send them a one-time email explaining the service and what to expect. We only contact them again if you miss a check-in and your grace period expires. They can opt out at any time by replying to that email.

Data retention

Your account and all associated data are deleted within 30 days of account deletion. Check-in history older than 365 days is automatically purged. Emergency contact information is deleted immediately when you remove a contact.

Self-hosted users

If you run Still Here on your own infrastructure, we have no access to your data. You are the data controller. Our Docker image contains no telemetry or phone-home code.

Security

Passwords are hashed with bcrypt. API keys are stored as SHA-256 hashes. JWTs use HS256 with a secret you control. Database connections use TLS. We do not store payment details — Stripe handles all card data.

Contact

Questions about privacy? Email us at hello@stillherehq.com. For self-hosted deployments, open an issue on GitHub.
